History of City of Dasmariñas

Home of Paru-Paro Festival

Dasmariñas City, located in the province of Cavite, has a rich history that dates back to the Spanish colonial period. Originally a small farming community sustained by agriculture and simple trade, it officially became a town on May 12, 1867, and was named after Governor-General José de la Cruz Dasmariñas. Over time, the town grew as settlers were drawn to its fertile land and opportunities. The people of Dasmariñas became known for their hardworking and resilient character, playing an important role during the Philippine Revolution against Spanish rule—an experience that helped shape the city’s strong sense of courage, dedication, and civic pride.

In modern times, Dasmariñas gained national recognition through the Paru-Paro Festival, an annual celebration symbolizing transformation, progress, and community unity through colorful street dances and artistic performances. Rapid growth in the late 1900s and early 2000s led to the development of schools, businesses, residential areas, and public infrastructure, reflecting the needs of its expanding population. This progress culminated in its official cityhood in 2009. Today, Dasmariñas stands as one of the most progressive cities in Cavite, successfully blending its rich historical heritage with modern development while offering cultural pride, educational opportunities, economic growth, and a hopeful vision for the future.
